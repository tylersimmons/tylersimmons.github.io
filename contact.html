Common Questions

Answers to things parents often ask.

What should my child bring to school each day?

A labeled backpack, a nut-free snack in a labeled bag, a water bottle, and a complete change of clothes. Everything should have your child's name on it!

What if my child is sick?

Please keep your child home if they have a fever (above 100.4°F), vomiting, diarrhea, or signs of illness. Your child should be symptom-free for 24 hours before returning.

How do I know what my child did in class?

Check the daily take-home folder! I send home notes or a weekly recap. You can also find updates on this website in the Announcements section.

My child is struggling with drop-off. What should I do?

Separation anxiety is very normal at this age! A quick, cheerful goodbye is usually best — lingering can make it harder. Your child almost always settles within minutes. Email me if you're concerned and we'll work through it together.

How can I volunteer in the classroom?

We love parent volunteers for special events! Keep an eye on announcements for opportunities. All volunteers need a background check through the school office first.

What is the school's allergy policy?

We are a nut-free classroom. Please do not send snacks containing peanuts or tree nuts. If your child has other allergies, please reach out so we can keep everyone safe.
