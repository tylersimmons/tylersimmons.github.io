📚 Reading at Home

Making storytime a joy for toddlers — every single day.

Reading aloud is one of the most powerful things you can do for your child's development. Even 10–15 minutes a day builds vocabulary, listening skills, and a love of stories that will last a lifetime. Here are some tips to make it fun:

  • Let your child choose the book — ownership builds excitement
  • Use silly voices for different characters
  • Point to pictures and ask "What do you see?"
  • It's okay to read the same book 10 times — repetition is how toddlers learn
  • Read before nap and bedtime to build a calming routine

🆕 Fun Activities at Home

Simple, engaging play ideas that support learning — no special supplies required.

🌳

Sensory Bins

Fill a container with dried pasta, rice, or kinetic sand. Add small toys or cups and let your child explore. Develops fine motor skills and focus.

🎨

Finger Painting

Cover the table with a trash bag and let them go wild! Finger painting builds creativity and strengthens hand muscles needed for writing.

🎶

Dance & Freeze

Play music and dance together — freeze when it stops! Builds body awareness, listening skills, and burns lots of toddler energy.

🧸

Playdough Play

Roll, squeeze, cut, and shape! Playdough strengthens hand muscles, supports creativity, and can keep a toddler happily busy for a long time.

🎲

Sorting & Counting

Sort socks by color, count crackers at snack time, or group toy cars by size. Early math is everywhere in everyday life!

🌎

Nature Walk

Take a slow walk and talk about what you see — bugs, leaves, clouds, textures. Nature walks build vocabulary and curiosity about the world.

📈 Developmental Milestones

A general guide to what to expect at ages 2 and 3. Every child grows at their own pace!

Around Age 2

  • Uses 2–3 word phrases ("more milk," "big dog")
  • Points to pictures in books when named
  • Imitates adults and older children
  • Shows excitement around other children
  • Runs (though may fall!), kicks a ball, climbs
  • Stacks 4+ blocks, turns pages in a book
  • Begins to sort shapes and colors

Around Age 3

  • Speaks in sentences of 4–5+ words
  • Understands "mine," "his," "hers"
  • Plays make-believe ("cooking," "driving")
  • Takes turns in games, shows concern for others
  • Pedals a tricycle, hops on one foot
  • Draws a circle, uses scissors with help
  • Works toys with buttons, levers, moving parts
Remember: These are general guidelines, not checklists. If you ever have questions or concerns about your child's development, please reach out — I'm always happy to chat, and we can connect you with school support resources if helpful.
